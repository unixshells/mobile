HOW TO TEST

1. Open the app and tap the "account" tab at the bottom.
2. Tap "Sign In" and enter username: demo (there is no password field — the app uses SSH key-based authentication, not passwords. For the demo account, simply enter "demo" as the username and tap Sign In.)
3. No SSH key selection or email approval is needed — the demo account signs in immediately.
4. Once signed in, you will see the account page with an active subscription, registered devices, and their sessions.
5. Tap the "shells" tab to see managed shell instances with their specs and status.
6. Swipe right (or tap the menu icon) to open the left drawer — this shows online relay devices and their active sessions. Tapping a session would normally connect via SSH to that device through our relay.
7. The "terminal" tab shows active terminal sessions. You can also create direct SSH connections by tapping the + button in the drawer.
8. The "settings" tab lets you configure terminal themes, fonts, manage SSH keys, and export/import backups.

ABOUT THIS APP

Unix Shells is a remote terminal client (SSH and Mosh). It does NOT pair with any physical hardware. All connections are to remote servers over the network via encrypted SSH sessions.

The "relay" feature lets users connect to machines behind NAT or firewalls via our cloud relay infrastructure — similar to a VPN or reverse tunnel. This is a cloud service, not a hardware accessory.

IN-APP PURCHASES

The Shell subscription ($3.99/month or $32.99/year) provides access to the Unix Shells relay infrastructure and managed shell environments. Direct SSH and Mosh connections to any server are free and do not require a subscription. The subscription can be purchased from the "shells" tab via the "New Shell" button.

To sign out of demo mode, go to the "account" tab and tap "Sign Out".
